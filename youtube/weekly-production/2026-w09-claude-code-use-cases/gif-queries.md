**Notes on spacing:**
- GIFs 1+2 are an intentional rapid-fire pair during the hook (the joke lands and then the judgment follows immediately)
- GIFs 3+4 cluster in Use Case 1 because the evolution-to-revenue arc is fast and needs both the speed and the payoff
- GIFs 7+8 are a deliberate skepticism-to-approval pair within Use Case 3
- No GIFs after 5:37 -- the Thesis, Surprise 6th, and CTAs are serious/reflective moments that should not be interrupted. The energy shifts from proof to philosophy, and GIFs would undercut the sincerity.

## Design Decisions

**Why no GIF in the hook (0:00-0:15)?** The opening is a rapid credibility stack ("SaaS, government system, cold email agent"). Interrupting it with a GIF would break the flow before the viewer is hooked.

**Why no GIF during the NFC "not a to-do app" line?** The speaker's delivery already carries the punch. A GIF would compete with the verbal emphasis rather than complement it. The jaw-drop at the 90% reveal (GIF 9) is the right place because the viewer has absorbed the claim and needs the visual punctuation.

**Why no GIF during the Anthropic/thesis section?** This is the philosophical core of the video. The speaker is making a genuine argument about focus vs. feature sprawl. GIFs here would signal "this is just entertainment" which contradicts the exact point being made.

**Why bottom-left for GIF 10 only?** The AEO section has a different visual layout (diagrams showing business -> system -> LLMs). Bottom-right would overlap with the content. Bottom-left keeps it visible without covering important information.
