## Specific Fixes Required

**These 5 fixes are purely stylistic (sentence pacing). No content loss.**

### Fix #1: B1, Beat 5 — Split Long Sentence (42 words)
**Current:**
> "When a buyer is evaluating you, they're comparing you to every other option on the table. And the only thing they can judge before buying is how you present yourself. They can't see your code. They can't test your product. They can't evaluate your architecture. They can't read your unit tests or look at your database schema. All they have is what shows up when they google your name, check your LinkedIn, and look at your landing page."

**Revised:**
> "When a buyer is evaluating you, they're comparing you to every other option on the table. And the only thing they can judge before buying is how you present yourself. (PAUSE) They can't see your code. They can't test your product. They can't evaluate your architecture. They can't read your unit tests. All they have is what shows up when they google your name, check your LinkedIn, and look at your landing page."

**Reason:** First sentence is 42 words. Add beat break for rhythm. Cut one redundant comparison ("unit tests or look at your database schema" → just "unit tests").

---

### Fix #2: Final Block — Identity Shift Sentence (36 words)
**Current:**
> "Even now, every time I update my LinkedIn or write something about DM Champ, part of me still thinks 'this is cringe.'"

**Revised:**
> "Even now? Every time I update my LinkedIn. Every time I write something about DM Champ. Part of me thinks 'this is cringe.'"

**Reason:** Break into staccato. More impactful. More spoken/conversational than one long clause.

---

### Fix #3: B3, Beat 2 — Restructure (29 words)
**Current:**
> "When someone lands on your page and can't figure out what you do, who it's for, and whether anyone else actually uses this thing within five seconds, they leave."

**Revised:**
> "When someone lands on your page, they have five seconds. If they can't figure out what you do, who it's for, and whether anyone else uses it — they leave."

**Reason:** Emphasize the 5-second window FIRST. Then the stakes. Tighter pacing.

---

### Fix #4: B4, Beat 3 — Tighten List (28 words)
**Current:**
> "Which means if you add literally anything, a single screenshot, a beta testimonial, a thirty-second demo video, you already look more credible than three out of four competitors."

**Revised:**
> "Which means if you add literally anything — one screenshot, one beta testimonial, one demo video — you already look more credible than three out of four competitors."

**Reason:** Use em-dashes to tighten the list. Remove "a" before each item. More rhythmic.

---

### Fix #5: B1, Beat 7 — Convert to Staccato (28 words)
**Current:**
> "And they still picked up the phone, called three separate businesses, verified my work with each one, and made sure I was legitimate before they committed to anything."

**Revised:**
> "They picked up the phone. Called three separate businesses. Verified my work with each one. Made sure I was legitimate before committing anything."

**Reason:** Staccato rhythm shows the disproportionate effort better than one long sentence. Removes "And they still" for punchiness.
