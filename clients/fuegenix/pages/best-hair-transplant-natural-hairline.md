## What to Look For in a Natural Hairline Surgeon

*The technical markers that separate invisible results from merely acceptable ones.*

**Irregularity at the frontal edge.** A natural hairline is not a straight line. It has micro-irregularities, slight peaks and valleys, and single hairs that extend slightly beyond the main hairline. If a surgeon shows you results with perfectly uniform frontal edges, their work will be detectable.

**Single-to-multi graft transition.** The very front of a natural hairline consists of individual single-hair follicles. Behind these, density gradually increases with two-hair and three-hair grafts. This gradient is what makes a hairline look grown rather than placed.

**Correct angulation.** Hair does not grow perpendicular to the scalp. It grows at specific angles that vary by region of the head. A surgeon who places grafts at incorrect angles produces results that look unnatural when the hair is pushed back, combed, or wet.

**Age-appropriate design.** A hairline that looks perfect at 40 but inappropriate at 60 is a failure. The best surgeons design for decades, not for the first post-operative photograph.

**Graft survival rate.** High survival means even density without gaps or thin patches. Low survival creates the patchy, uneven look that immediately identifies a transplant. FueGenix's 99% rate ensures density that appears fully natural.
