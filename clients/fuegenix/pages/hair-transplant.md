## Techniques Section

**Headline:** `Advanced Techniques`

**Subheadline:** `The right method for your specific situation.`

**Image:** `[technique-comparison-fue-sapphire-dhi.png]` `Alt: Comparison of FUE, Sapphire FUE, and DHI hair transplant techniques`

### FUE (Follicular Unit Extraction)

**Paragraph 1:** `FUE is the foundation of modern hair transplantation. Individual follicular units are extracted from the donor area and transplanted to the recipient zone without linear scarring. Dr. Ahmad has performed thousands of FUE procedures, refining his technique to maximize graft survival and aesthetic outcome.`

**Key Benefits:**
- No linear scar in donor area
- Faster recovery than strip methods
- Suitable for most hair types
- Can harvest from beard and body if needed

### Sapphire FUE

**Paragraph 1:** `Sapphire FUE uses blades crafted from synthetic sapphire crystal rather than steel. These blades create smaller, more precise incisions that heal faster and allow for higher density placement. Dr. Ahmad employs Sapphire FUE for clients seeking maximum density with minimal recovery time.`

**Key Benefits:**
- Smaller incision channels
- Reduced trauma to scalp
- Faster healing
- Higher density achievable
- Less post-operative edema

### DHI (Direct Hair Implantation)

**Paragraph 1:** `DHI uses a specialized implanter pen to simultaneously create the recipient site and place the graft. This reduces the time grafts spend outside the body and allows for precise control over angle, depth, and direction. Dr. Ahmad selects DHI for specific cases where these advantages are paramount.`

**Key Benefits:**
- Grafts spend less time outside body
- Precise control over placement angle
- Ideal for hairline work
- Can be performed without shaving

## The Artistry Section

**Headline:** `The Philosophy of Invisible Art`

**Subheadline:** `Why the best results are the ones no one notices.`

**Image:** `[natural-vs-artificial-hairline.png]` `Alt: Side-by-side comparison of natural hairline with irregularity vs artificial pluggy hairline`

**Paragraph 1:** `A "perfect" hairline is immediately suspect. Nature does not produce perfect lines. Natural hairlines have micro-irregularities, subtle temporal recession, and variation in density. When a hairline is too perfect, too straight, too uniform, it announces itself as artificial.`

**Paragraph 2:** `Dr. Ahmad's artistry lies in creating hairlines that incorporate natural imperfection. The goal is not to create a hairline that looks good. The goal is to create a hairline that looks like it was always there.`

**Key Principles:**

**Natural Irregularity:**
`Every natural hairline has subtle variation. Dr. Ahmad designs hairlines with appropriate irregularity, avoiding the straight-line effect that signals work was done.`

**Facial Harmony:**
`A hairline must complement facial structure. Age, face shape, existing hair characteristics, and individual features all inform the design. There is no template; each design is unique.`

**Angulation Mastery:**
`Natural hair emerges at specific angles that vary across the scalp. Correct angulation is invisible in photos but obvious in person. It allows hair to move naturally and be styled in any direction.`

**Image:** `[hair-angulation-diagram.png]` `Alt: Technical diagram showing correct 15-35 degree hair angulation vs incorrect 90 degree placement`

**Density Calibration:**
`More is not always better. Excessive density in the wrong areas creates an unnatural appearance. The art is knowing exactly how much density serves the result and where.`
